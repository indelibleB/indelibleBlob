**Target Segment:** Field Researchers, Environmental Scientists, Data Scientists  
**Market Size:** $100B+ annual research funding  
**Phase:** Phase 2 (Months 25-36) - Requires spatial data capture  
**Pricing:** $19/month (Academic), $10k-$50k/year (Institutional)

## USER: Who Has the Problem?

### Persona 1: Environmental Scientist / Field Researcher
- Conducts field research (climate, ecosystems, geology)
- Collects spatial data, photos, measurements
- Publishes in peer-reviewed journals
- $50k-$90k/year salary (university or government)
- Grant-funded research

**Pain:** Data integrity questioned, reproducibility crisis, publication requirements for data verification

---

### Persona 2: GIS Analyst / Spatial Data Scientist
- Analyzes spatial datasets
- Creates visualizations, maps, models
- Works with government agencies, NGOs, universities
- $60k-$100k/year salary
- Needs verified, reproducible datasets

**Pain:** Data provenance unclear, quality inconsistent, reproducibility challenges

---

### Persona 3: University / Research Institution
- Funds research programs
- Publishes research findings
- Subject to data integrity requirements
- Budget: $1M-$100M+ research funding
- Reputation depends on research integrity

**Pain:** Replication crisis, data fraud scandals, funder requirements for data verification

---

## PROBLEM: What Painful Problem Do They Experience?

### Core Problem

**Scientific research faces a data integrity and reproducibility crisis. Field data provenance is unclear, datasets can't be verified, and publications are retracted due to data fraud. Funders (NSF, NIH) now require data verification, but researchers lack tools to prove data authenticity at capture. This threatens careers, grants, and scientific progress.**

---

### Problem Layers

**Layer 1: Reproducibility Crisis**
- 70% of researchers unable to reproduce others' experiments
- Data provenance unclear (where, when, how collected)
- No verification of field data authenticity
- **Impact:** Scientific progress slowed, trust eroded

**Layer 2: Publication Requirements**
- Journals requiring data verification for publication
- Reviewers questioning data authenticity
- No standard for field data verification
- **Impact:** Papers rejected, careers stalled

**Layer 3: Grant Funding Requirements**
- NSF, NIH requiring data management plans
- Funders demanding data transparency
- No tools for field data verification
- **Impact:** Grants rejected, research unfunded

**Layer 4: Data Fraud & Retractions**
- High-profile data fraud scandals
- Papers retracted (career-ending)
- No way to prove data wasn't fabricated
- **Impact:** Reputation damage, career loss

---

## SOLUTION: How Does indelible.Blob Solve the Problem?

### Core Solution

**indelible.Blob verifies field research data at capture using GPS, blockchain, and Walrus storage. Researchers prove WHERE data was collected, WHEN, and that it hasn't been altered. This meets publication and funder requirements, prevents data fraud, and enables reproducible science.**

---

### Solution Components

**1. Verified Field Data Capture**
- GPS proves location of data collection
- Timestamp proves when
- Device metadata proves what instruments used
- Blockchain creates immutable record
- **Benefit:** Provable data provenance

**2. Spatial Data Verification (Phase 2)**
- LiDAR integration for environmental monitoring
- Photogrammetry for site documentation
- GPS + orientation for spatial accuracy
- **Benefit:** Verified spatial datasets

**3. Publication-Ready Data**
- Verification badge for papers
- Data availability statements with blockchain proof
- Reviewer verification interface
- **Benefit:** Papers accepted, not rejected

**4. Grant Compliance**
- Meets NSF/NIH data management requirements
- Funder verification interface
- Institutional licenses for universities
- **Benefit:** Grants funded, compliance achieved

---

### Competitive Differentiation

**vs. Manual Documentation:** Automated vs. error-prone  
**vs. Lab Notebooks:** Digital + blockchain vs. paper  
**vs. Data Repositories:** Verification at capture vs. after collection  
**vs. Nothing:** Proactive verification vs. no verification
