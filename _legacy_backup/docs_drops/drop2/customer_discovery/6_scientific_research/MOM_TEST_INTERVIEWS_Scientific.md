# The Mom Test Interviews: Scientific Research Segment
## 5-7 In-Depth Customer Discovery Sessions

**Duration:** 45-60 minutes  
**Target:** Field researchers, university administrators  
**Goal:** Validate data integrity problem, solution fit, pricing

---

## Key Questions

### Data Integrity Questions

**Q1: "Tell me about your most recent field research project. What data did you collect?"**
- Listen for: types of data, collection methods, challenges

**Q2: "Have you ever had your data questioned by reviewers, colleagues, or funders?"**
- Listen for: specific experience, how it affected publication/grant

**Q3: "Walk me through how you document data provenance (where, when, how collected)."**
- Listen for: lab notebooks, metadata, GPS logs, manual processes

---

### Reproducibility Questions

**Q4: "Have you tried to reproduce another researcher's work? What happened?"**
- Listen for: success/failure, data availability, provenance issues

**Q5: "Are you concerned about data fraud or fabrication in your field?"**
- Listen for: awareness of problem, personal concerns

---

### Solution Demo

> "We're building field data verification using GPS + blockchain. Proves where/when data was collected and that it hasn't been altered. Creates verification badge for publications."

**Q6: "Would this have helped with the data challenge you described?"**
- Listen for: YES (validates solution), specific value

**Q7: "Would verified data help with your next grant application or publication?"**
- Listen for: grant compliance, publication requirements

---

### Pricing Questions

**Q8: "What's your annual research budget? How much goes to data management?"**
- Listen for: grant size, data management allocation

**Q9: "We're considering $19/month for individual researchers or $10k-$50k/year for universities. Does that fit your budget?"**
- Listen for: academic pricing sensitivity, institutional interest

---

### Commitment Test

**Q10: "Would you use this on your next field research project?"**
- Listen for: YES (strong signal), requirements

---

## Success Criteria

1. **Problem Severity:** 5/7 have had data questioned or are concerned
2. **Solution Fit:** 5/7 would use indelible.Blob for field data
3. **Pricing (Academic):** 4/7 would pay $19/month
4. **Pricing (Institutional):** 2/3 universities would pay $10k-$50k/year
5. **Next Project:** 4/7 would use on next field research project
