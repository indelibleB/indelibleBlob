# Finding Interest: Insurance Segment
## 10 Conversations to Validate Fraud Prevention Demand

**Target:** Insurance adjusters, fraud investigators, claims executives  
**Goal:** Validate that $40B fraud problem creates demand for proactive verification  
**Time:** 10-15 minutes per conversation, 10 conversations total

---

## Who to Talk To

**Recommended Mix:**
- 4 Field Adjusters (auto, property, commercial)
- 3 Fraud Investigators (SIU specialists)
- 2 Claims Executives (VP Claims, Chief Risk Officer)
- 1 InsurTech Professional (technology consultant, industry analyst)

**Why This Mix:**
- Adjusters experience fraud daily (front-line pain)
- Investigators quantify fraud costs (ROI validation)
- Executives make buying decisions (budget authority)
- InsurTech validates market trends

## Conversation Script (10-15 minutes)

### Opening (1 minute)

> "Thanks for taking the time! I'm [Your Name], researching fraud prevention in insurance claims. I'm NOT trying to sell you anything—I'm learning from people who actually do this work. I have a few questions about photo verification and fraud challenges. Does that sound okay?"

---

### Discovery Questions (8-10 minutes)

**Q1: "Tell me about your role. What types of claims do you typically handle?"**
- Listen for: claim volume, types (auto, property, etc.), workflow
- This builds rapport and context

**Q2: "Have you ever encountered a fraudulent claim where photos were manipulated or taken at a different time/location?"**
- Listen for: specific stories, frequency, how they discovered it
- Follow-up: "What happened? How did you handle it?"
- **This validates the problem exists**

**Q3: "How do you currently verify that photos submitted with claims are authentic?"**
- Listen for: current tools, manual processes, time spent
- Follow-up: "How well does that work? What's frustrating about it?"
- **This reveals gaps in current solutions**

**Q4: "With AI making it easier to generate or manipulate images, how has that changed your approach to claims?"**
- Listen for: increased skepticism, new verification steps, concerns
- Follow-up: "Do you worry about fraudsters using AI to create fake evidence?"
- **This validates urgency**

**Q5: "If you could wave a magic wand and solve one problem related to photo verification in claims, what would it be?"**
- Listen for: their ideal solution (may not match yours)
- **This reveals what they value most**

---

### Interest Gauge (2-3 minutes)

**Transition:**
> "That's really helpful. So, I'm working on a tool that verifies photos at the moment of capture using GPS and blockchain. It creates an immutable record proving when and where the photo was taken, and that it hasn't been manipulated. Does that sound like something that would be useful for your work?"

**If YES:**
- "What would make this worth paying for?"
- "Would your company be interested in a pilot program?"
- "Can I add you to a waiting list for updates?"

**If NO:**
- "What concerns do you have?"
- "What would make it more useful?"

**If MAYBE:**
- "What questions do you have?"
- "What would you need to see to be convinced?"

---

### Closing (1-2 minutes)

> "This has been incredibly helpful. Thank you! A few quick things:
> 1. Would it be okay if I follow up as we develop this?
> 2. Do you know anyone else in insurance who might be interested in chatting about this?
> 3. If we build a pilot program, would you be willing to test it?"

**Capture:**
- ✅ Email/LinkedIn contact
- ✅ Company name and role
- ✅ Referrals (2-3 names if possible)
- ✅ Permission to follow up

## Red Flags 🚩

**🚩 "We already have fraud detection software"**
- **What it means:** They think current tools are sufficient
- **What to do:** Ask what tools they use, what works, what doesn't—learn if it's truly solving the problem

**🚩 "Fraud isn't a big problem for us"**
- **What it means:** Either they're in denial or they have unusually low fraud
- **What to do:** Ask how they measure fraud, what their loss ratio is—may reveal hidden problem

**🚩 "This would require IT approval and integration"**
- **What it means:** Long enterprise sales cycle, integration complexity
- **What to do:** Ask about their technology stack, procurement process—plan for 6-12 month sales cycle

**🚩 "Adjusters won't change their workflow"**
- **What it means:** Change management challenge
- **What to do:** Emphasize ease of use, ask what would make adoption easier

**🚩 "We'd need to see ROI data first"**
- **What it means:** They're risk-averse, need proof
- **What to do:** Offer pilot program, ask what metrics would prove ROI

---

## Green Lights ✅

**✅ "We lose millions to fraud every year"**
- **What it means:** Problem is severe and quantified
- **What to do:** Get specific numbers, ask for pilot program

**✅ "Our current verification process is manual and slow"**
- **What it means:** They're frustrated with status quo
- **What to do:** Demo how indelible.Blob automates verification

**✅ "How much would this cost? When can we start?"**
- **What it means:** High purchase intent
- **What to do:** Discuss enterprise pricing, pilot program timeline

**✅ "You should talk to our VP of Claims"**
- **What it means:** They see value and want decision-maker involved
- **What to do:** Ask for warm introduction

**✅ "Can we pilot this with 20 adjusters?"**
- **What it means:** Ready to move forward
- **What to do:** Capture requirements, timeline, success criteria

## Success Criteria

**After 10 conversations:**

1. **Problem Validation:** 7/10 have encountered fraud with manipulated photos
2. **Severity:** 5/10 say fraud costs company $1M+/year
3. **Interest:** 7/10 express interest in proactive verification solution
4. **Willingness to Pay:** 4/10 would pay $10k-$25k/year per adjuster
5. **Pilot Interest:** 2/10 would pilot with 10-50 adjusters

**If 4/5 criteria met:** Proceed to in-depth interviews (The Mom Test)

**If 2-3 criteria met:** Refine messaging, do 5 more conversations

**If 0-1 criteria met:** Consider pivoting to different segment or different problem
