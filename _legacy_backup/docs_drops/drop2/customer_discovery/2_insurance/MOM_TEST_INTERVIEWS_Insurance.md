# The Mom Test Interviews: Insurance Segment
## 5-7 In-Depth Customer Discovery Sessions

**Duration:** 45-60 minutes per interview  
**Target:** Insurance adjusters, fraud investigators, claims executives  
**Goal:** Validate problem severity, solution fit, pricing, and pilot readiness

---

## Interview Structure (6 Phases)

### Phase 1: Context & Rapport (5-7 min)
Build trust, understand their world

### Phase 2: Problem Discovery (15-20 min)
Learn about fraud challenges, current solutions, pain points

### Phase 3: Solution Exploration (10-15 min)
Demo indelible.Blob, gauge reaction, identify objections

### Phase 4: Commitment Test (5-10 min)
Test willingness to pay, pilot program interest

### Phase 5: Feature Prioritization (5-7 min)
Understand must-have vs. nice-to-have features

### Phase 6: Next Steps (3-5 min)
Referrals, follow-up, pilot planning

---

## Phase 1: Context & Rapport (5-7 min)

### Opening

> "Thanks so much for making time! Before we dive in, I want to be clear: I'm NOT here to sell you anything today. I'm genuinely trying to learn from people who deal with insurance fraud daily. My goal is to understand your challenges so we can build something that actually solves real problems—not just what we think the problems are. Sound good?"

### Context Questions

**Q1: "Tell me about your role at [Company]. What does a typical day look like?"**
- Listen for: workflow, tools, team structure, volume
- Follow-up: "How many claims do you handle per week/month?"

**Q2: "How long have you been in insurance? What brought you to this field?"**
- Listen for: experience level, career path, motivations
- This builds rapport and reveals expertise

**Q3: "What's the most challenging part of your job?"**
- Listen for: pain points (may or may not be fraud-related)
- This reveals priorities

---

## Phase 2: Problem Discovery (15-20 min)

### The Mom Test Principle
**Ask about PAST BEHAVIOR, not hypothetical futures.**
- ✅ "Tell me about the last time you encountered a fraudulent claim"
- ❌ "Would you use a tool that verifies photos?"

### Fraud Experience Questions

**Q4: "Walk me through the last fraudulent claim you encountered. What happened?"**
- Listen for: specific details, how they discovered it, outcome
- Follow-up: "How did you know it was fraudulent?"
- Follow-up: "What evidence tipped you off?"
- **This validates the problem is real**

**Q5: "How often do you encounter suspicious or fraudulent claims?"**
- Listen for: frequency (daily, weekly, monthly)
- Follow-up: "What percentage of your claims would you estimate are fraudulent?"
- **This quantifies severity**

**Q6: "Tell me about a time when you suspected fraud but couldn't prove it. What happened?"**
- Listen for: frustration, limitations of current tools, consequences
- Follow-up: "What would you have needed to prove it?"
- **This reveals solution requirements**

### Current Solution Questions

**Q7: "Walk me through how you currently verify that photos in a claim are authentic."**
- Listen for: specific steps, tools, time spent, reliability
- Follow-up: "How long does that process take?"
- Follow-up: "How confident are you in the results?"
- **This reveals gaps in current solutions**

**Q8: "What tools or software do you use for fraud detection?"**
- Listen for: specific products, costs, satisfaction level
- Follow-up: "What works well? What's frustrating?"
- **This identifies competitors and pain points**

**Q9: "Have you ever paid for forensic image analysis? Tell me about that experience."**
- Listen for: cost, timeline, effectiveness, decision process
- Follow-up: "Was it worth the cost?"
- **This validates willingness to pay for verification**

### Impact Questions

**Q10: "What does a fraudulent claim cost your company? Walk me through the impact."**
- Listen for: direct costs, indirect costs, time, reputation
- Follow-up: "How does this affect you personally?"
- **This quantifies pain and personal stakes**

**Q11: "Tell me about a time when approving a fraudulent claim had consequences for you or your team."**
- Listen for: performance reviews, warnings, job security concerns
- Follow-up: "How did that make you feel?"
- **This reveals emotional pain (stronger motivator than financial)**

---

## Phase 3: Solution Exploration (10-15 min)

### Demo Setup

> "Based on what you've shared, I want to show you what we're building and get your honest reaction. Remember—I'm not trying to sell you anything today. I want to know if this actually solves your problem or if we're way off base."

### Demo Flow (5 minutes)

**Step 1: Show mobile app**
- "Adjuster opens indelible.Blob app instead of camera"
- "Takes photo of damaged vehicle at claim site"

**Step 2: Show verification**
- "GPS automatically records location (accurate to 1-3 meters)"
- "Timestamp recorded on Sui blockchain (immutable)"
- "Photo stored on Walrus (can't be altered)"

**Step 3: Show verification interface**
- "Anyone can verify: Where was this taken? When? Has it been modified?"
- "Cryptographic proof, not just metadata"

**Step 4: Show claims integration**
- "Photo automatically uploads to your claims system with verification data"
- "Verification status visible in dashboard"

### Reaction Questions

**Q12: "What's your immediate reaction to this?"**
- Listen for: excitement, skepticism, questions, objections
- **This gauges initial interest**

**Q13: "How would this change your workflow? Walk me through it."**
- Listen for: adoption barriers, workflow fit, training needs
- Follow-up: "What would make it easier to adopt?"
- **This reveals implementation challenges**

**Q14: "What concerns do you have about this approach?"**
- Listen for: privacy, cost, integration, reliability, legal admissibility
- Follow-up: "What would address that concern?"
- **This identifies objections to overcome**

**Q15: "If you had this tool six months ago, how would it have changed that fraudulent claim you told me about?"**
- Listen for: specific value, whether it would have prevented/detected fraud
- **This validates solution fit**

---

## Phase 4: Commitment Test (5-10 min)

### The Mom Test Principle
**Test commitment with REAL ACTIONS, not hypothetical interest.**
- ✅ "Would you pilot this with 10 adjusters next quarter?"
- ❌ "Would you be interested in this?"

### Pricing Questions

**Q16: "Your company currently spends money on fraud detection—forensic analysis, investigators, etc. How much would you estimate you spend annually?"**
- Listen for: budget range, decision-makers, procurement process
- **This anchors pricing discussion**

**Q17: "If this tool could prevent even 10% of fraud, what would that be worth to your company?"**
- Listen for: ROI calculation, value perception
- Follow-up: "What would you personally be willing to pay per adjuster per year?"
- **This validates pricing**

**Q18: "We're considering pricing this at $10,000-$25,000 per adjuster per year, or $100,000-$500,000 for company-wide deployment. How does that compare to what you currently spend on fraud prevention?"**
- Listen for: sticker shock, comparison to alternatives, budget fit
- Follow-up: "Is that in the ballpark of what you'd expect?"
- **This tests pricing directly**

### Pilot Program Questions

**Q19: "If we built a pilot program for 10-20 adjusters, would you be interested in participating?"**
- Listen for: YES (strong signal), MAYBE (lukewarm), NO (not interested)
- **This tests real commitment**

**Q20: "What would need to be true for you to say YES to a pilot program?"**
- Listen for: requirements, timeline, success criteria, decision-makers
- Follow-up: "Who else would need to approve this?"
- **This reveals buying process**

**Q21: "If the pilot showed a 10-20% reduction in fraudulent claims, would you roll it out company-wide?"**
- Listen for: commitment level, decision process, timeline
- **This validates long-term potential**

---

## Phase 5: Feature Prioritization (5-7 min)

### Feature List

Present these features and ask them to rank:

1. **GPS verification** (proves location)
2. **Blockchain timestamp** (proves when)
3. **Immutable storage** (can't be altered)
4. **Claims system integration** (API)
5. **Mobile app** (easy capture)
6. **Web dashboard** (verification interface)
7. **Legal admissibility** (court-ready evidence)
8. **Forensic analysis** (AI-powered fraud detection)

### Prioritization Question

**Q22: "Of these features, which 3 are MUST-HAVES for you to adopt this? Which are nice-to-have?"**
- Listen for: priorities, deal-breakers, feature gaps
- Follow-up: "Why is [feature] so important?"
- **This guides product roadmap**

**Q23: "What's missing from this list? What feature would make this a no-brainer for you?"**
- Listen for: unmet needs, competitive gaps
- **This reveals product opportunities**

---

## Phase 6: Next Steps (3-5 min)

### Referrals

**Q24: "Who else in the insurance industry should I talk to about this?"**
- Listen for: 2-3 specific names
- Follow-up: "Would you be willing to introduce me?"
- **This expands your network**

### Follow-Up

**Q25: "Can I follow up with you as we develop this? I'd love to keep you in the loop."**
- Listen for: YES (engaged), MAYBE (lukewarm), NO (not interested)
- **This maintains relationship**

### Pilot Planning

**Q26: "If we move forward with a pilot program, what would the timeline look like? What's your procurement process?"**
- Listen for: 3 months, 6 months, 12 months, decision-makers, budget cycles
- **This sets expectations**

### Closing

> "This has been incredibly valuable. Thank you for your time and honesty. I'll send you a summary of what we discussed and keep you updated on our progress. And if you think of anything else, please reach out anytime!"

## Success Criteria

**After 5-7 interviews:**

1. **Problem Severity:** Average score 7+/10 (fraud is severe problem)
2. **Solution Fit:** Average score 7+/10 (indelible.Blob solves problem)
3. **Pricing Validated:** 4/7 say $10k-$25k/year is acceptable
4. **Pilot Interest:** 3/7 would pilot with 10-20 adjusters
5. **Must-Have Features:** GPS + Blockchain + Integration ranked top 3

**If all 5 criteria met:** Build pilot program, begin enterprise sales

**If 3-4 criteria met:** Refine solution, do 3 more interviews

**If 0-2 criteria met:** Pivot to different segment or different solution

## Common Objections & Responses

### Objection 1: "This sounds expensive"

**Response:** "I hear you. Let me ask—how much does your company lose to fraud annually? And how much do you currently spend on forensic analysis per suspicious claim?"

**Goal:** Reframe cost as investment with clear ROI

---

### Objection 2: "Adjusters won't change their workflow"

**Response:** "That's a valid concern. Tell me more about their current workflow. What would make a new tool easy or hard to adopt?"

**Goal:** Understand adoption barriers, design for ease of use

---

### Objection 3: "We already have fraud detection software"

**Response:** "That's great! What software do you use? How well does it work for photo verification specifically?"

**Goal:** Identify gaps in current solutions

---

### Objection 4: "This would require IT approval and integration"

**Response:** "Absolutely. What's your technology stack? What claims management system do you use? We're building API integrations for Guidewire, Duck Creek, and others."

**Goal:** Demonstrate integration capability, understand requirements

---

### Objection 5: "How do I know this is legally admissible?"

**Response:** "Great question. Blockchain evidence is increasingly accepted in courts. Can you tell me about a time when evidence admissibility was challenged in one of your cases?"

**Goal:** Understand legal requirements, plan for legal validation

## Key Learnings to Capture

After each interview, document:

1. **Problem Insights:** What surprised you about their fraud challenges?
2. **Solution Gaps:** What features are they asking for that we don't have?
3. **Pricing Feedback:** Is $10k-$25k/year too high, too low, or just right?
4. **Buying Process:** Who makes decisions? What's the timeline?
5. **Competitive Intel:** What other solutions are they considering?
6. **Objections:** What concerns came up repeatedly?
7. **Quotes:** Capture powerful quotes for marketing/sales
