# Finding Interest: Activists & Whistleblowers Segment
## 10 Conversations to Validate Anonymous Verification Demand

**Target:** Human rights activists, environmental activists, NGOs  
**Goal:** Validate need for anonymous evidence verification  
**Time:** 10-15 minutes per conversation

---

## Who to Talk To

- 4 Human Rights Activists
- 3 Environmental Activists / Watchdogs
- 2 NGO Directors (Human Rights Watch, Amnesty, etc.)
- 1 Investigative Journalist (covers activism/whistleblowing)

---

### NGOs

## Conversation Script

### Opening (Sensitive Topic)
> "I'm researching evidence verification for activists and whistleblowers. Would you be willing to chat about documentation challenges? Your identity will remain confidential. No sales pitch—just learning."

### Key Questions

**Q1: "Have you documented misconduct (abuses, pollution, corruption)?"**
- Listen for: YES (validates use case), types of documentation

**Q2: "Has your evidence ever been dismissed as fabricated or manipulated?"**
- Listen for: specific experiences, frustration

**Q3: "Do you need anonymity for safety reasons?"**
- Listen for: YES (validates privacy requirement), threats faced

**Q4: "How do you currently balance anonymity with evidence credibility?"**
- Listen for: trade-offs, challenges, no good solution

**Q5: "Would anonymous verification (prove authenticity without revealing identity) be valuable?"**
- Listen for: YES/NO, concerns

---

## Success Criteria

1. **Problem:** 7/10 have had evidence dismissed
2. **Safety:** 7/10 need anonymity
3. **Interest:** 6/10 would use anonymous verification
4. **NGO Interest:** 3/3 NGOs interested in organizational license
5. **Phase 2 Timing:** 7/10 would wait for Seal privacy layer (2026)
