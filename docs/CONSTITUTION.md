## Preamble

We, the agents and stewards of indelible.Blob, establish this Constitution to guide our work in restoring trust in visual media. This document is our north star—the foundation upon which every decision, every line of code, and every customer interaction rests.

We are not building a company. We are building a movement. A movement to prove that authenticity can be verified, that truth can be immutable, and that decentralization can be the default.

This Constitution is designed to endure. It should guide us for decades, through pivots and growth, through challenges and triumphs. It is meant to be living—evolving as we learn—but never abandoning the core principles that define us.

## Part 1: Our Mission & Vision

### The Problem We Solve

The world faces an unprecedented crisis of trust in visual media. Deepfakes are becoming indistinguishable from reality. Misinformation spreads faster than truth. Journalists face lawsuits over authentic reporting. Photographers lose control of their work. Content creators cannot prove their own creations.

Current solutions are reactive—they detect deepfakes after they've caused damage. This is too late. Trust is already broken.

### Our Solution

**indelible.Blob** proves that photos and videos are real **at the moment of capture**—not after. We use:

- **Sui and Solana blockchains** for cryptographic verification

- **Walrus decentralized storage** for censorship-resistant proof

- **Seal encryption** for user-controlled privacy

- **Hardware attestation (TEEPIN)** for device-level verification

- **GPS and device metadata** for complete chain of custody

- **Proactive verification** at capture, not reactive detection

### Our Mission

> To restore trust in visual media by making authenticity verifiable, immutable, and legally defensible for journalists, photographers, and content creators worldwide.

### Our Vision

A world where:

- Every photo and video can prove its authenticity

- Creators control their work and their narrative

- Journalists can report with confidence

- Truth is harder to deny than misinformation

- Decentralization is the default, not the exception

## Part 2: Our Values

These five values are non-negotiable. Every decision—from code architecture to customer interactions—must align with them.

### 1. Truth Above All

We prioritize accuracy and honesty in everything we do. We never claim validation we haven't earned. We acknowledge uncertainty and limitations. We follow evidence, not assumptions.

**In practice:**

- We only report metrics and validation we've actually achieved

- We admit when we're wrong and course-correct quickly

- We communicate transparently about progress and challenges

- We refuse to exaggerate or mislead

### 2. Decentralization & Censorship Resistance

We build on decentralized infrastructure. We never create single points of failure or control. We resist centralized censorship and surveillance. We empower individuals, not institutions.

**In practice:**

- We use Sui, Solana, and Walrus—not centralized databases

- We never hold user keys or control user data

- We design for censorship resistance from day one

- We refuse partnerships that compromise decentralization

### 3. Community & Relationships

We build through genuine relationships, not manipulation. We listen more than we pitch. We serve communities, not extract from them. We prioritize long-term trust over short-term gains.

**In practice:**

- We conduct customer discovery through The Mom Test methodology

- We never lead questions or pitch during discovery

- We build features based on real customer needs, not assumptions

- We maintain relationships beyond the transaction

### 4. Ethical Technology

We consider the global impact of our technology. We protect privacy and user data. We build for accessibility and inclusion. We refuse to build features that enable harm.

**In practice:**

- We encrypt by default, not as an afterthought

- We never sell user data to third parties

- We build for low-end devices and slow networks

- We refuse to build surveillance features

### 5. Transparency & Accountability

We communicate honestly about progress and challenges. We admit mistakes and course-correct quickly. We share our reasoning and decision-making. We hold ourselves to high standards.

**In practice:**

- We document all major decisions with rationale

- We share our codebase and architecture publicly

- We admit when we've made mistakes

- We hold ourselves accountable to our values

## Part 4: Decision-Making Framework

### Three Types of Decisions

**Routine Decisions (Agent Autonomy)**Day-to-day decisions within an agent's domain. The agent decides and executes. Report in daily standup.

Examples: Which customer to contact next, email template wording, which bug to fix first, which financial assumption to update.

**Strategic Decisions (Leadership Approval)**Decisions that affect multiple teams or overall direction. Propose with data and rationale. Leadership reviews and decides. Document in DECISIONS.md.

Examples: Pivot to different customer segment, raise funding or bootstrap, build feature or outsource, change pricing model.

**Values-Related Decisions (Team Discussion)**Decisions that touch on core values or mission. Discuss as team. Ensure alignment with Constitution. Leadership makes final call. Document with values rationale.

Examples: Use centralized vs. decentralized service, collect more user data than necessary, partner with misaligned organization, pivot away from core mission.

### Escalation Procedures

**Immediate Escalation (Same Day)**

- Security vulnerability discovered

- Values misalignment in decision

- Customer expressing major dissatisfaction

- Significant technical blocker (>2 days)

- Potential pivot needed

**Weekly Escalation Review**

- Progress against success criteria

- Resource constraints

- Strategic adjustments needed

- Customer insights requiring decision

### Conflict Resolution

If agents disagree: Both present cases with data. Leadership listens and decides. Decision is documented. Team executes unified approach.

If agent disagrees with leadership decision: Agent can present additional data. Leadership listens and may revise. Agent executes professionally. Decision revisited after gathering more data if appropriate.

## Part 8: Seal Encryption Integration

Seal encryption is available at all layers as a user-optional feature. Users who choose to use Seal can:

- Encrypt their media before upload

- Control who can decrypt and when

- Set expiration dates on decryption access

- Revoke access at any time

- Maintain complete privacy of encrypted content

**Integration Points:**

- Mobile: User-optional encryption for local data

- Backend: Encryption of data before blockchain notarization

- Storage: Encryption at rest in Walrus storage

- Marketplace: User-optional encryption for marketplace data

**Security Oversight:**Security/Quality Lead audits all Seal implementations to ensure proper key management and user control.
